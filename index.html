Earning Spain

Running, walking, and rucking through Spain on a Rick Steves tour — balancing personal effort with shared cultural discovery.

This journey unfolded on foot, before breakfast runs and long guided walks, under pack weight and under cathedral ceilings. From Roman coastlines to medieval hill towns, Spain was experienced not just by seeing it, but by moving through it — step by deliberate step.

Tarragona

The journey began independently, before the tour schedule and before the headsets. Tarragona offered a quiet entry into Spain — Roman stone overlooking the Mediterranean, sea air moving through ancient ruins.

Before the tour. Before the headsets.

Barcelona

Barcelona blended motion and art effortlessly. Early miles traced the curve of the Mediterranean while the city slept. Later steps slowed through Gothic streets and Gaudí’s improbable geometry.

Miles, mosaics, Mediterranean light.

NEXT →

Madrid

Madrid carried weight — physical, political, and historical. Morning runs cut through Retiro Park in quiet contrast to the city’s grand scale. Walking tours stretched across plazas built for empire.

Capital miles. Measured in plazas.

NEXT →

Toledo

The route ended uphill. Toledo rose above the Tagus River, compact and steep, layered with centuries of convergence and conflict. Each climb felt earned. Each view felt final.

Climbing toward closure.
